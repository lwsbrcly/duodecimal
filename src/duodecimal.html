Duodecimal Watchface Configuration

Watchface Components

First hand.
Rotates once per day. Midnight is straight down, sunrise on the left, noon is straight up and sunset on the right.

Second hand. (optional)
Rotates 12 times quicker than the previous hand. i.e. 1 rotation = 2 decimal hours.

Third hand. (optional)
Rotates 12 times quicker than the previous hand. i.e. 1 rotation = 10 decimal minutes.

Fourth hand. (optional)
Rotates 12 times quicker than the previous hand. i.e. 1 rotation = 50 decimal seconds.

Show decimal time. (optional)
To keep it old school, if your brain starts to overheat...

Show duodecimal time. (optional)
Numeric rendering of the 4 hands. Upsidedown 2 is the new "ten". Upsidedown 3 is the new "eleven". Duodecimal now means that the number 27, for example, is 2 dozens + 7 units. That equates to 31 in decimal. Just sayin'...

Subsecond rendering. (optional)
In order to feel the full sweeping power, leave this on. To conserve a tiny amount of battery, you can return to the old "tick tock" of updating once per decimal second.
